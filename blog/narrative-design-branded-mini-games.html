Narrative Design Pillars for Branded Mini-Games

Storytelling is what transforms a branded experience from a campaign asset into a memorable journey. We design narratives that honor the brand, empower players, and adapt across languages. These pillars guide our story teams from pitch deck to localization QA.

Theme

Define an emotional anchor aligned with brand promise.

Characters

Develop multi-dimensional protagonists and supporting cast.

Structure

Plan beats for onboarding, rising tension, climax, and resolution.

Localization

Build modular copy and art ready for regional adaptation.

Author

Lior Hazan

Anchor Every Mission in Theme

We start with a single thematic sentence, such as "Curiosity uncovers hidden talents" or "Generosity sparks community." Every mission, collectible, and challenge references this theme through dialogue, UI copy, and art direction. Players feel consistent emotional cues regardless of level.

Write Characters With Agency

Branded games often rely on mascots, but agency matters more than mascots. We give protagonists decisions that alter the experience: choosing how to approach a puzzle, which ally to recruit, or what reward to prioritize. Non-playable characters provide mentorship without stealing the spotlight.

Character Toolkit

  • Motivation triangle (personal, communal, brand-aligned).
  • Signature phrases and gestures that reinforce tone.
  • Accessibility notes covering pronunciation, reading level, and cultural context.

Storyboard the Journey

Our team uses four-act structures to map pacing. Act I introduces the world and mechanics. Act II complicates the mission with new twists. Act III delivers a climactic challenge that tests mastered skills. Act IV celebrates the player and offers epilogues or bonus quests.

Storyboards align writers, artists, and engineers. We annotate each frame with dialogue beats, UI states, and audio cues so production can scale smoothly.

Localize With Respect

Localization is more than translation. We supply context files with cultural references, idioms, and tonal guidelines. Voice-over scripts include pronunciation guides, while art layers separate text elements for quick swaps. Regional consultants review early builds to flag cultural mismatches.
