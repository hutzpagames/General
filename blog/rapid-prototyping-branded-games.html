Rapid Prototyping for Campaign Games

Speed unlocks creativity. Our rapid prototyping framework gets brands from idea to playable demo in as little as two weeks. Learn how we align stakeholders, pick the right tools, and validate mechanics before investing in full production.

Week 1

Game pillars, paper prototypes, and playtest scripts.

Week 2

Week 3

Week 4+

Stakeholder validation, scope locking, production roadmap.

Uri orchestrates lightning-fast sprints that turn campaign briefs into validated prototypes, aligning marketers, creatives, and engineers around the same build.

Kickoff With Pillars and Risks

We begin every sprint by defining three pillars that describe the experience (e.g., "cooperative problem solving" or "bite-sized mastery"). Alongside the pillars we document risks—technical, narrative, and operational. This clarity keeps the team focused when decisions get tough.

Prototype in Layers

Our workflow moves from analog to digital quickly: paper sketches, interactive wireframes, then HTML5 or Unity vertical slices. Each layer introduces fidelity without discarding learnings from the previous step.

Tooling Stack

  • Miro for storyboarding mechanics and flows.
  • Figma for interactive UI/UX prototypes.
  • PlayCanvas or Phaser for HTML5 builds accessible in browsers.
  • Lookback or Maze for remote playtest recordings.

Test With Real Players Early

By Day 5 we run moderated playtests with 6-8 participants representing target personas. Sessions focus on comprehension, enjoyment, and brand recall. We gather both quantitative scores and qualitative notes, then adjust the prototype overnight.

The process repeats until we reach a "confident yes" from players and stakeholders. Only then do we lock scope and build a production roadmap.
